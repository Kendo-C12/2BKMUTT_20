### INSTITUTIONAL CONTEXT: KMUTT (BANGMOD)
King Mongkut's University of Technology Thonburi, also known as "Bangmod," is a prominent Thai university focused on engineering, technology, and innovation. The university's official symbol is the "Ant." KMUTT operates two primary campuses: the main campus located in Thung Khru and the Bang Khun Thian campus, which houses the Faculty of Architecture and Design. A key initiative is the "2B-KMUTT" program, a research camp designed to provide high school students with direct experience in university-level research and innovation projects.

### SYSTEM CONTEXT: TELESORTING SYSTEM
The TeleSorting System is a remote-controlled educational tool used for learning real-time programming and industrial automation. It allows users to observe immediate physical machine responses to programmed commands. The system is used within KMUTT's curriculum and research programs, such as 2B-KMUTT, to demonstrate the integration of software and physical hardware.

### HARDWARE AND SENSING COMPONENTS
The TeleSorting unit consists of three primary hardware categories:
1. Optical Proximity Sensors: Used to detect the presence of a workpiece on the conveyor belt.
2. Vision Sensors: Used specifically for color detection and identification of workpieces.
3. Linear Actuators: Mechanical components that push or redirect workpieces to change their transport direction.

### COMMUNICATION AND ARCHITECTURE
The system architecture relies exclusively on the MQTT Protocol for all remote operations. This protocol enables a "Tele" (remote) connection between the user's control logic and the physical sorting unit. 
- Device Identification: Every sensor and actuator is assigned a specific ID number.
- Data Flow: Users interact with the system by reading data from Input IDs (representing sensors) and publishing commands to Output IDs (representing actuators).
